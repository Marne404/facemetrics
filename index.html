What sets FaceMetrics apart

An analysis that measures — not flatters.

Most rating apps reward good lighting and a straight head. FaceMetrics factors both out and scores what actually belongs to the face.

Pose-invariant

Full 3D frontalization removes roll, yaw and pitch from the landmark cloud before any measurement. A head turned 15° yields the same score.

Facial fat & double chin

Calibrated lower-face fullness metrics plus a silhouette trace of the profile photo detect submental fat — the factor front-only apps simply miss.

Metric-by-metric breakdown

Every area shows its individual measurements against a calibrated ideal band — as Health-app style bars, each with its own 0–10 sub-score. Legible, not magic.

Iris millimetre scale

The human iris is almost universally 11.7 mm wide — a built-in ruler. Real millimetres (pupil distance, cheekbone and mouth width) become measurable from a single photo.

100% on-device

Landmarks, scoring and skin analysis run entirely on your phone via MediaPipe. Your photos never leave the device — there is no upload and no server behind it.

Apple-grade design

Adaptive light & dark on the iOS system palette, an Activity ring for every score, spring animations and grouped lists — the same language you're looking at right now.

Three steps

From photo to report.

1

Pick your photos

A straight front photo is enough. For jaw angle, nasal projection and the double-chin contour, add an optional profile shot — it's checked for identity against the front photo.

2

Deep scan

478 landmarks are placed, the face is straightened in 3D, expression is removed via blendshapes, and the rating is averaged across 14 jittered passes.

3

PSL report

An overall score with tier and percentile, eight rated areas with expandable measurements, and an honest ± margin — plus a note when the photo limits accuracy.

Privacy first

Your photos stay with you.

FaceMetrics was built as a pure on-device app. There is no photo upload, no cloud analysis and no tracking of your facial data.

  • No upload. Landmark detection and scoring run locally through MediaPipe.
  • GDPR consent on first launch, with a clear statement of purpose.
  • Open source. Every line is verifiable on GitHub.
